Understanding UK Aggregate Measurements

Before you can calculate anything, you need to understand how aggregate is measured in the UK. This is where many people get confused, but it is actually straightforward once you know the basics.

Volume (Cubic Metres) vs Weight (Tonnes)

When you order aggregate, you are usually buying by weight, either in tonnes or bulk bags. But to know how many tonnes you need, you first need to calculate the volume of space you are filling.

Volume is measured in cubic metres (m³). It is simply: Length × Width × Depth.

Example:

If you are covering an area 5 metres long, 4 metres wide, and 0.1 metres deep (100mm), your volume is: 5m × 4m × 0.1m = 2 cubic metres (2 m³)

Bulk Density Explained

Bulk density is how much a cubic metre of a material weighs. It varies depending on the type of aggregate. Here are the typical densities I use on-site for common UK materials:

Material Bulk Density (t/m³) Common UK Uses
MOT Type 1 / Hardcore 1.9 – 2.2 Driveway sub-base, road foundations, hardstanding
Gravel / Pea Shingle 1.5 – 1.7 Drainage, paths, decorative ground cover
Topsoil 1.3 – 1.5 Landscaping, garden beds, lawn preparation
Building Sand 1.4 – 1.6 Mortar, bricklaying, plastering
Sharp Sand 1.5 – 1.7 Screeding, block paving, concrete mixes
General Ballast 1.7 – 1.9 Concrete mixing, foundations
Slate Chippings 1.2 – 1.4 Decorative garden paths, driveways

Why does this matter? Because 2 cubic metres of MOT Type 1 weighs a lot more than 2 cubic metres of topsoil. If you do not account for density, your weight calculation will be wrong, and you will either under-order or over-order.

How to Calculate Aggregate Requirements (Step-by-Step)

Over the years, I have developed a simple, reliable method for calculating aggregate needs. I use this same process on every project, and it is exactly what our calculator automates for you.

Step 1: Measure Your Area

Start by measuring the length and width of the area you are covering. Use a tape measure and record your measurements in metres (or feet if you are using imperial units). For irregular shapes, break them down into rectangles, calculate each area, and add them together.

Step 2: Determine the Depth

This is where experience matters. The depth you need depends entirely on your project. Here are the depths I recommend based on years of practical work:

  • Driveways (MOT Type 1): 100–150mm (0.1–0.15m)
  • Paths (Gravel): 50–75mm (0.05–0.075m)
  • Patios (Base Layer): 75–100mm (0.075–0.1m)
  • Garden Beds (Topsoil): 50–100mm (0.05–0.1m)
  • Concrete Foundations: 100–150mm (0.1–0.15m)

Pro Tip:

Always check with your supplier or a structural engineer if you are unsure, especially for load-bearing applications like driveways. For official UK construction standards, refer to the NHBC Standards for site-mixed concrete, which provide detailed guidance on mix proportions and aggregate requirements.

Step 3: Calculate Volume

This is the simple part: Length × Width × Depth = Volume (m³)

Example: A driveway 10m long, 5m wide, with a depth of 0.1m: 10 × 5 × 0.1 = 5 cubic metres (5 m³)

Step 4: Account for Compaction

Here is a mistake I see all the time: people forget that aggregate compacts. When you lay MOT Type 1 for a driveway and roll it with a compactor, it settles. If you order exactly the volume of loose material, you will end up short.

The rule I use: Add 10% extra for compaction. For loose material that will be compacted, multiply your volume by 1.1.

Example: 5 m³ × 1.1 = 5.5 m³

Step 5: Add Waste Allowance

No matter how careful you are, there is always waste, spillage, uneven ground, or material that gets left behind. I recommend adding 5–10% waste allowance.

  • Simple, flat areas: 5% is usually enough.
  • Complex shapes or uneven ground: Add 10%.

Example: 5.5 m³ × 1.1 = 6.05 m³

Step 6: Convert Volume to Weight

Now we convert cubic metres to tonnes. Use the density of your chosen material.

Formula: Volume (m³) × Density (t/m³) = Weight (tonnes)

Example (MOT Type 1, density 2.05 t/m³): 6.05 m³ × 2.05 = 12.40 tonnes

Step 7: Calculate Cost

Finally, multiply the weight by your supplier's price per tonne. In the UK, MOT Type 1 typically costs between £12 and £25 per tonne, depending on your location and quantity.

Example: 12.40 tonnes × £20 = £248.00

Common Aggregate Projects in the UK

Over the years, I have helped homeowners and contractors with countless projects. Here is how to calculate for the most common ones.

Driveways (MOT Type 1)

MOT Type 1 is the standard sub-base material for driveways in the UK. It is crushed stone that compacts well and provides excellent drainage.

Typical depth: 100–150mm (0.1–0.15m) | Density: 2.05 t/m³ (average)

Example: 10m × 5m driveway, 100mm depth

Volume: 10 × 5 × 0.1 = 5 m³ → With compaction (10%): 5.5 m³ → With waste (10%): 6.05 m³ → Weight: 6.05 × 2.05 = 12.4 tonnes

Gravel Paths and Patios

Gravel is popular for paths and decorative areas. It is lighter than MOT Type 1 and easier to work with.

Typical depth: 50–75mm (0.05–0.075m) | Density: 1.6 t/m³ (average)

Example: 8m × 3m path, 50mm depth

Volume: 8 × 3 × 0.05 = 1.2 m³ → With waste (5%): 1.26 m³ → Weight: 1.26 × 1.6 = 2.02 tonnes

Garden Landscaping (Topsoil)

When you are creating new garden beds or preparing for turf, topsoil is essential. It is the lightest of the common aggregates.

Typical depth: 50–100mm (0.05–0.1m) | Density: 1.4 t/m³ (average)

Example: 12m × 4m garden bed, 100mm depth

Volume: 12 × 4 × 0.1 = 4.8 m³ → With waste (5%): 5.04 m³ → Weight: 5.04 × 1.4 = 7.06 tonnes

How to Save Money on Aggregate Purchases

After helping hundreds of clients order materials, I have learned a few tricks to save money without cutting corners.

  • Buy in bulk when it makes sense: If you need more than 10 tonnes, ordering a full tipper lorry is usually cheaper per tonne than buying bulk bags.
  • Compare supplier prices: MOT Type 1 typically £12–25 per tonne, Gravel £10–20 per tonne, Topsoil £15–30 per tonne.
  • Consider recycled aggregates: Recycled MOT Type 1 is often cheaper (around £8–15 per tonne) and performs just as well for non-structural applications.
  • Account for delivery costs: Delivery costs can add £50–100 to your order. Ordering more tonnes in one delivery reduces the cost per tonne.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much MOT Type 1 do I need for a driveway?

For a standard driveway, calculate the area (length × width), multiply by depth (0.1–0.15m), add 10% for compaction, and 5–10% for waste. Then multiply by density (2.05 t/m³). A 10m × 5m driveway needs about 12–13 tonnes.

What is the difference between compacted and loose aggregate?

Loose aggregate is freshly delivered material before it is compacted. When you compact it with a roller or wacker plate, it settles by about 10%. Always add 10% extra if you are compacting.

How many bulk bags do I need for 1 tonne?

One bulk bag typically holds 800kg (0.8 tonnes). For 1 tonne, you will need 1.25 bags, so order 2 bags if you need more than 0.8 tonnes.

Can I use this calculator for gravel paths?

Yes. Use a depth of 50–75mm and select "Gravel" from the material list. Add 5% waste for simple paths.

What is the typical price of MOT Type 1 in the UK?

Prices vary by region, but typically range from £12 to £25 per tonne. Recycled MOT Type 1 can be cheaper (£8–15 per tonne).
